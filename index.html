    Using Principle Component Analysis to Rank and Scout NBA Players

    Project Completed by: Jackson Bronkema

    During my final semester studying mathematics at Taylor University, I was able to conduct semester long research on a topic of my choice. Being a sports fan, expecially from a business analytics perspective, I choose to use a linear algebra concept called Principle Component Analysis to analyze the true worth of NBA players. During this project, I collected data on current players and all-time great players to rank them. I also took a financial application of these rankings and adivsed NBA teams on how they should use analytics to decide on which players they should sign in free agency and the NBA draft. I was also able to present my research to a live audience on math majors and professors, which was very rewarding!
